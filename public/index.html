What this form captures

The screening combines early symptom burden, anxiety indicators, body metrics, and psychosocial stressors into a single probability estimate.

Why this matters

A low threshold is used here as a screening choice to prioritize sensitivity, making the tool more suitable for early follow-up discussion.

What the result means

The output is best interpreted as a probability-based risk signal, not a diagnosis. It should support referral and follow-up decisions rather than replace clinical judgment.

About the model

Portfolio-level framing so the site reads like a complete project, not only a form.

What the model does

This classifier estimates the probability of postpartum depression risk using first-trimester demographic, clinical, and psychosocial features. It is designed for screening-oriented use rather than definitive diagnosis.

How it works

Inputs are preprocessed and mapped into a logistic regression pipeline. The model then outputs a probability, which is compared against a low threshold to support sensitivity-oriented early screening.

Why this site matters

The project demonstrates end-to-end work: machine learning, deployment, UI design, model communication, and research-oriented content design in a single interface.
